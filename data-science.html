Mathematics and Descriptive Statistics

Principles of Statistics

Definition and Objectives

  • Statistics: The science that deals with processes for collecting, presenting, and interpreting data effectively.
  • Objective: To provide information about data under analysis for a better understanding of the represented facts.
  • Descriptive statistics: Initial stage of data analysis aimed at describing observed data.

Data and Collection

Data: Can be obtained from a population or a sample.

  • Population: Set of elements sharing at least one common characteristic.
  • Sample: Representative subset of a population.

Elements and Variables

  • Element: Each unit observed in the study.
  • Variable: Characteristic observed, measured, or counted in the elements of a population or sample.
    • Qualitative variable: Takes values of attributes or qualities (categorical).
    • Quantitative variable: Takes numerical values.

Sampling

  • Random: each element of the population has an equal chance of being selected.
  • Systematic: a random selection of the first element of the sample is made, and then the remaining elements are chosen using fixed or systematic intervals.
  • Stratified: separates the elements into homogeneous groups and then a random sample is taken from each group (stratum).
